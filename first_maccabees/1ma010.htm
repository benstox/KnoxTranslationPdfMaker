    [5] It seems possible that the word \xe2\x80\x98Demetrius\xe2\x80\x99 may have been inserted for the sake of clearness; Josephus treats Apollonius throughout as fighting on the side of Alexander (in spite of verse 88).

    \n

    [6] \xe2\x80\x98All the time he was luring them on into the plain\xe2\x80\x99; this seems to be the meaning of the Greek text, although Joppe and Azotus were both on the sea-board, far away from any hill-country. The Latin has \xe2\x80\x98immediately he went out into the plain\xe2\x80\x99, which yields no satisfactory sense.
